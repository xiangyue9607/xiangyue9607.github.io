Bio

I am an AI researcher in the TBD Lab at Meta Superintelligence Labs (MSL). Before joining Meta, I spent two wonderful years at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU) as a postdoctoral researcher, working with Prof. Graham Neubig on natural language processing (NLP) and large language models (LLMs). I received my Ph.D. in 2023 from The Ohio State University‚Äôs OSU NLP Group, where I was advised by Prof. Huan Sun and Prof. Yu Su. I completed my B.S. in Computer Science at Wuhan University.


My research aims to understand and enhance the reasoning capabilities of LLMs across different modalities and contexts:
  • Understanding LLMs' reasoning through rigorous evaluation and benchmarking (e.g., MMMU/MMMU-Pro/MMLU-Pro, Demystifying Long CoT, GrokkedTransformer, Transferability of LLM Reasoning)
  • Improving LLMs' reasoning capabilities with synthetic data generation (e.g., MAmmoTH/MAmmoTH2/MAmmoTH-VL, Pangea, OpenCodeInterpreter)
